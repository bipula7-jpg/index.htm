June 4, 2026 — What Happened Today

Today's defining story is a sharp sector rotation: investors sold AI chip stocks after Broadcom missed revenue estimates ($22.19B vs $22.27B expected) and kept its full-year AI chip guidance flat at $100B — a number the market had priced in being raised. AVGO fell 12.6%, dragging the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index down 2.8%.

Where did the money go? Healthcare (+3.1%) and Financials (+1.8%) — sending the Dow to a fresh all-time high of 51,562. UnitedHealth alone surged 5.2% after Bank of America upgraded it to Buy with a $450 target. JPMorgan rose 3%, Blackstone rebounded 7.5%.

The bigger macro picture: Fed rate hike probability jumped from 60% to 85% for year-end this week after ADP private payrolls beat at +122K and JOLTS job openings hit their highest since November 2024. With PCE at 3.5% from energy inflation and the Iran conflict keeping oil elevated, the Fed has little room to cut — and may need to hike. The next FOMC is June 16-17.
